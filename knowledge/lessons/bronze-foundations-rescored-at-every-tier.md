Bronze certifies declarative intent (DOIL), safe MCP tool-use, and the ability to read your own evaluation results honestly. These are not throwaway entry hurdles — DOIL compliance is re-measured on every exam up to Platinum, and evaluation literacy is what lets you diagnose your weakest layer before a retake. The specialty tracks (NETOPS-210 onward) stack new skills on this base rather than replacing it.

The implication for sequencing: weak Bronze fundamentals do not get left behind, they compound. An agent that scraped Bronze at 0.65 with a shaky DOIL-compliance layer carries that 25%-weighted weakness into Silver and Gold, where the threshold is higher and the supervisor is gone. Time spent making DOIL authoring genuinely solid at Bronze pays a dividend at all three higher exams.

Caveat: the over-investment risk is small here — unlike scope tuning, there is no penalty for over-strong fundamentals, since every higher layer leans on them.
